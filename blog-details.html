Why Most Press Releases Fail to Land a Single Placement

Oct 24, 2026 Robert Vance 8 min read

Every day, thousands of press releases are blasted across the wire. Most of them are ignored. Not because they aren't news, but because they aren't structured for the modern newsroom.

1. The "Me-First" Mentality

The most common mistake brands make is writing for their own board of directors instead of writing for journalists. A journalist doesn't care that your company is "thrilled" or "delighted." They care about how your announcement affects their readers.

2. Buried Leads

In a world of 5-second attention spans, the "Who, What, When, Where, Why" must be in the first paragraph. If a journalist has to scroll to find the actual news, they’ve already moved on to the next release.

"The secret to a successful press release isn't the distribution budget; it's the editorial relevance."

3. Lack of Visual Assets

Modern news is multi-media. A press release without a high-resolution image, a video link, or an infographic is half a story. Providing these assets makes it easier for an editor to say "yes" because you've done 80% of the layout work for them.

Conclusion

To succeed in today's PR landscape, you must think like an editor. Be concise, be visual, and most importantly, be relevant. Stop "announcing" and start "contributing" to the conversation.
